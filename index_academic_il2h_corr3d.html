Methodological overview

Overview of the IL2H model and clustering algorithm

Based on the theoretical structure of IL2H, the algorithm proceeds in three phases: identifying candidate causal clusters, refining those clusters, and correcting edge assignments within the recovered latent structure.

Phase 1Finding causal clusters
Using a greedy search, this phase detects clusters of observed variables likely sharing a latent ancestor. It performs rank-deficiency tests on subsets of variables. The active set Sa ⊆ XG starts as the full observed variable set and shrinks as latent covers are identified.
Phase 2Refining clusters
Clusters obtained in Phase 1 may be inaccurate due to the greedy heuristic, especially when they correspond to bond sets or subsets of variables that induce spurious clustering. Corrective operations are used to remove bond sets and obtain latent atomic covers that better reflect the underlying latent structure.
Phase 3Refining edges
Having identified latent clusters, this phase corrects edge assignments within clusters so that conditional independence assumptions are met. It addresses residual dependencies among children of latent nodes or incorrect parent assignments.
Connection to this visualisation: the 3D display shows the recovered latent-item structure, while the orientation stage illustrates why the final interpretation is parallel dimensionality rather than a stable hierarchy.
